The method

The PIVOT Method™

You don't think your way into a new career — you act your way into one. Our framework is built on identity research and a simple principle: reinvention isn't a leap, it's a series of small, smart, reversible experiments that compound into a new direction.

P

Profile — map who you are now

Audit your real assets: transferable skills, domain expertise, values, financial runway. We separate your identity from your job title, so losing the title never feels like losing yourself.

I

Identify — find the viable pivots

We don't chase one fantasy job. We generate a portfolio of 3–5 plausible target careers, scored on fit, 2026 demand, AI-resilience, and how close they are to your current skills.

V

Validate — test before you leap

Small, real-world experiments: informational interviews, a side project, a short course, a freelance gig. You gather evidence instead of guessing. Most fears shrink the moment they meet reality.

O

Operationalize — build the bridge

The de-risking plan: timeline, income runway, the skills to acquire (including the AI fluency your target role needs), and a reframed résumé and narrative. "Someday" becomes a dated plan.

T

Transition — make the move and land it

We position your story so a hiring manager sees a 15-year asset, not a beginner. Interview strategy, salary negotiation, and a first-90-days plan so the new role actually sticks.

Straight answers

The questions you're already asking

Aren't I too old to change careers?
No — you're too experienced to start over, which is different. The fear of "missing the window" assumes you'd begin from zero. You wouldn't. People change careers in their 40s and 50s successfully every day. What stops most isn't age — it's not having a credible plan and trying to do it alone at midnight. That's the part we fix.
I can't afford a pay cut. Doesn't a pivot mean less money?
That's exactly why the program exists. The whole strategy is engineered around the salary bridge — moving careers at your current income or higher by negotiating from experience, not desperation. If a pay cut were the only path, you wouldn't need us.
I already work 50 hours a week. Where's the time?
The roadmap runs on nights and weekends, an hour at a time — without quitting your job or tipping off your employer. You don't have to find time for everything. We hand you the single next step, so you never waste an hour on the wrong move.
How do I know AI won't just take the new job too?
Because we don't guess. We match you to roles that are growing because of AI — where human judgment, relationships, and accountability are the product — not roles in line to be replaced. We show you the reasoning before you commit to a direction.
Do I need to be technical or learn to code?
No. Most of the highest-demand AI-era roles reward judgment, communication, and domain expertise — not coding. We'll map exactly the level of AI fluency your specific target role needs, and build it into your plan.
I've tried career stuff before and nothing changed.
Most of it is generic advice and personality quizzes aimed at confused 22-year-olds. This is a 1:1 plan built around your experience, your income floor, and a specific target role, with a coach who has worked in that field. Not inspiration — a map.
